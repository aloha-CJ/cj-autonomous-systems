**2. Crypto as Property = Capital Gains Treatment**
- Cryptocurrency treated as property (not currency) for tax purposes
- **Fair Market Value (FMV) at receipt = income and cost basis**
- Example: Receive $1,500 in Bitcoin ‚Üí $1,500 income, $1,500 basis
- Later sale creates capital gain/loss: Sale price minus cost basis
- Mining/earning crypto = taxable income on Form 1099-NEC at FMV

**3. Instant Fiat Conversion = Tax Simplification**
- **Merchants auto-converting to fiat = just report normal business income**
- No capital gains tracking needed if instant conversion
- "Merchant does not need to accept crypto" (conversion happens in background)
- PayPal: "Instant crypto to stablecoin or fiat conversion" available
- Simplifies accounting dramatically (eliminates volatility + tax complexity)

**4. Holding Crypto = Tracking Nightmare**
- If merchant holds crypto: Must track cost basis for every transaction
- "Any gain or loss when cryptocurrency is later exchanged or sold must be reported"
- Multiple transactions = multiple basis calculations
- Volatility creates unpredictable tax liability
- Software like Koinly, TokenTax needed to track properly

**5. Strategic Tax Approaches**
- **Best for merchants:** Instant fiat conversion (zero tax complexity)
- **Stablecoins:** Minimize volatility, simplify accounting
